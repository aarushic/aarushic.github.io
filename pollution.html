Physical Pollution

About the Theme

There's trash in the seas, trash in the forests, trash that will last as long as our world will. Plastic, paper, cardboard, and glass, some of the most used materials in the world, are recyclable, but only around 9% of recycled materials get recycled. This is even more astonishing considering how 75% of American waste is recyclable. Numerous factors contribute to these low numbers, from how we manage our waste systems to the unavailability of recycling. This has to change, as recycling helps reduce greenhouse gas emissions by reducing energy consumption. Tune in for Day 2 of our conference to learn how!

This Day's Events

Ioan M Magee (Morning)

Born in Bulgaria, Ioan has been passionate about the effects of climate change since a young age, in his native area. Ioan is the founder of Bulgaria For Change, a now nationally known non-profit organization seeking to increase awareness of pollution, in its physical forms, in his country and the world. Known for helping youths in lesser-known communities organize large-scale projects on trash pickup, water cleanup, and more locally centered restoration, this organization has spread widely. He has been a great voice in this fight for sustainability and advocating for green policies.

Osbeorn I Howard (Evening)

Originally from Greater Manchester, England, Osbeorn comes from a long generation of people fighting against climate change, more specifically land pollution and its effects on the ecosystem. Specializing in the field of ecology, Howard has made significant contributions in the field. Speaking at the UN Climate action conference, and being the co-founder of Earth Comes First, a non-profit organization raising awareness of the effect of pollution and climate change on more than just humans.

Recycling Competition

To raise awareness, we’ve come up with a fun way to recycle. By recycling materials into art! Gather all the recyclable materials you can find, and create whatever your imagination provides you. You can use plastic bottles, cardboard, glass, even paper bags. Work in teams, individually, in a large-scale project, or even something that takes you 5 minutes to complete, as long as you have fun! To make this even more fun, post it on TikTok, and gather views. Just remember to add our hashtag and post within the time frame of the event! May the most viewed TikTok, aka, best recycler win!

  • Three minutes or less in length. Videos longer than this will not be considered.
    No exceptions!
  • Only recyclabe materials can be used.
  • Use the hashtag #theresnoearthwithoutart to tag your video.
  • Tune into our awards ceremony at 5:00 PM to see who the winner is!

Trash Cleanup

We’ve also made helping a whole lot easier and more fun! Organize a trash pickup or clean-up day in your neighborhood or the local area, with friends, family, or a group of people. Send us a plan of what you would like to accomplish and we will send you the things you need and get you in contact with a member of our organization to help you out. At the end of the day, all we ask of you is to send us info on how much trash you’ve collected and a picture for our records and shoutouts! Unlike most events, this one will last for a while, so contact us when you want and help make a difference for you and the people around you!

Green Awakening

Green Awakening is a climate conference dedicated to teaching others about the devastating effects of climate change and what we can do to combat it. Our mission is to ensure that our planet will be habitable for our future generations.

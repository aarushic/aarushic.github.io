Here’s a preview of a few of the global projects we’ve done and will be talking about at the conference.

What We Do

As a nonprofit organization, we have taken on numerous projects all over the world to advocate for the fight against climate change and to help those affected by the consequences of global warming.

Climate Literacy

One of our biggest projects is about teaching kids about climate change. We send teams of volunteers and scientists that work with us to schools around the world. Our team shares climate change statistics with school age children to highlight how pressing this issue is. We also teach them about actions that they can take to reduce their own carbon footprint.

Reforestation Program

This project is all about combating deforestation. We send our employees and local volunteers to rainforests and forests around the world to help repopulate them. We provide all the necessary supplies to our groups to ensure that they are as successful as they possibly can.

Regenerative Agriculture

This initiative allows our organization to work with farmers to teach them about regenerative agriculture. Regenerative agriculture practices include no till agriculture, composting, using biochar, and planting perennial plants when possible. These practices improve soil health by increasing organic matter, which increases biodiversity and carbon drawdown (taking carbon out of the atmosphere).

Global Trash Cleanup

We employ different groups of volunteers all over the world that clean land near them. Any group can sign up with us and we provide them with instructions of how to properly clean the land and what to do with the waste. We have employees in charge of each project so our organization personally oversees each cleanup.
